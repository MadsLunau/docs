The onboarding task has built your Customer Agent: its personality, policies, knowledge, and standard ticketing tools are already in place. From here, this page takes you from your first resolved ticket to a support setup that keeps getting better. Each level is a stage; move to the next whenever you're ready.

| Level | Your agent | What you get |
| :-- | :-- | :-- |
| **1** | Answer correctly | Fast, consistent, on-brand replies |
| **2** | Answer correctly, personalized to the customer | Answers that fit each person's account |
| **3** | Act correctly on the customer's behalf | More tickets resolved end to end |
| **4** | Turn customer tickets into fewer future tickets | Fewer tickets over time |

## Level 1: Answer correctly

Most of this is already set up. Here's what's left on your end.

### Get your first ticket resolved

<Steps>
  <Step title="Review your configurations">
    Open the "Customer agent" tab and look over what was built for you, or ask the Manager Agent (called "Internal agent" in the app today) to walk you through it. See [Configurations](/customer-agent/configurations).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Test it on your common scenarios">
    Ask the Manager Agent to test the tickets you see most, like refunds or a troubleshooting flow. You'll see the reply, the reasoning, and every tool call. If something looks off, ask for a change and test again. See [Testing](/customer-agent/testing).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Go live">
    Happy with the responses? Set up a workflow in your ticketing system that assigns tickets to Parahelp. See [Ticketing systems](/customer-agent/channels/ticketing-systems).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Audit the first tickets">
    Start with a subset. Check outcomes in [Reporting](/customer-agent/reporting), or ask the Manager Agent to QA any ticket.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Keep its knowledge fresh, automatically

Answers stay right only if knowledge stays current. Set up [automations](/manager/automations) like:

- Update knowledge from Slack, Linear, Notion, GitHub, and human-handled tickets
- QA tickets with bad-info flags or low CSAT
- Send you a daily or weekly performance report

<Tip>
  You don't have to map the next step yourself. As the Manager Agent works your tickets and reports, it creates todos for the next improvement, with the context attached and assigned to the right person, ready to start in one click from the Tasks page (rolling out in beta).
</Tip>

## Level 2: Know the customer

Once answers are reliably right, connect customer data so the agent knows who it's talking to.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Give it read access">
    Ask the Manager Agent to build read tools for your database, billing system, or CRM. It builds and tests each one, then stages it for your approval.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set the rules">
    Tell it when to look up customer data and what's in scope. It writes those policies into your agent's memory.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Test with real accounts">
    Run your common scenarios again, across different plans and payment histories, and confirm the answers match each customer's account.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Level 3: Take action

Now let your agent do things, not just explain them.

<Steps>
  <Step title="List what your team does by hand">
    Refunds, cancellations, plan changes, account updates. Anything your team clicks through today.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Turn each one into a tool">
    Describe it to the Manager Agent. Your rules get enforced in code, like refund windows and amount limits, and you can require Slack approval before anything runs.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Approve and watch the first runs">
    Every tool lands in [Actions](/manager/actions) for your review before going live.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Level 4: Fewer tickets

Your tickets are a signal. Set up [automations](/manager/automations) that:

- Spot bug patterns, verify them in your codebase, and file them in your tracker
- Spot feature request patterns and send them to product
- Catch potential incidents early and alert the right team

Questions along the way? Ask the Manager Agent, or check the [FAQ](/resources/faq).
